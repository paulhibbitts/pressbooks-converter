Convert a Pressbooks XHTML export to a Grav pages folder for Grav Helios Open Reader

A web-first reading experience for your Pressbooks content — Grav Helios Open Reader is a focused, self-hosted reader purpose-built for the web, with Save My Place, LMS embedding, and content stored as portable Markdown files.

  1. 1
    From your Pressbooks dashboard, click Export in the left sidebar. In the Export Options panel, check XHTML and click Export Book. Alternatively, use the Download this book dropdown on the book cover page. Download the .html file.
  2. 2
    Upload that file below and click Convert & Download ZIP. A pages/ folder structure ready for Grav will be downloaded.
  3. 3
    Extract the ZIP and copy the pages/ folder into your Grav Helios Open Reader installation.
  4. 4
    In Grav Admin, go to Themes → Helios → Versioning → Version Labels and update each section label to match your book's section titles — these labels control the section card titles on the site. The included versioning-labels.yaml in the ZIP contains ready-to-paste labels for your book.

What exactly does this do?

It's a self-contained PHP web app running on the server. You upload a Pressbooks XHTML export, it parses the structure and converts everything to Markdown, then hands you back a ZIP ready to drop into Grav.

What's under the hood?
PHP 8+ with DOMDocument/DOMXPath for HTML parsing · league/html-to-markdown for HTML → Markdown conversion · ZipArchive for packaging · Vanilla HTML/CSS/JS frontend — no framework, no build step.

What happens to your file?

  1. Your upload is validated server-side — file type, size (50 MB max), and structure are all checked before anything runs
  2. The book structure is parsed: metadata, front matter, parts, chapters, and back matter are all extracted, along with internal links
  3. Each page's HTML is cleaned up — footnotes, videos, definition lists, callout blocks, and links are all handled before conversion to Markdown
  4. A pages/ folder structure is assembled with proper YAML frontmatter and a ready-to-paste versioning-labels.yaml
  5. The ZIP is streamed back to your browser and the temporary file is immediately deleted from the server

What's in the ZIP?
Numbered section-N/ folders, each with a section.md and numbered section-page.md files for every chapter · A versioning-labels.yaml with your book's section titles ready to paste into Grav · Image downloads are optional; Cloudflare-protected hosts can't be downloaded server-side.
